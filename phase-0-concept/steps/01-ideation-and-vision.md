# Step 1: Ideation & Vision

## Purpose

Distill a raw idea into a clear, communicable vision. By the end of this step, anyone should be able to read your Vision Statement and understand what the app does, who it's for, and why it matters.

## Process

### 1.1 Capture the Raw Idea

Write the idea down in its roughest form. Don't filter or refine — just get it out of your head and onto paper. This is the "napkin sketch" moment.

**Prompt yourself:**
- What sparked this idea?
- What would the app do in one sentence?
- What's the core interaction? (What does the user *do* most often?)

### 1.2 Define the Problem Statement

Every good app solves a problem or fulfills a desire. Articulate what that is.

**Framework:**
> "[Target audience] struggle with [problem]. Currently, they [current workaround]. This results in [negative outcome]."

If you can't fill this in, the idea might be a solution looking for a problem. That's not always fatal, but it's a yellow flag.

### 1.3 Identify Target Audience

Be specific. "Everyone" is not an audience.

**Define:**
- **Primary audience:** Who benefits most from this app?
- **Secondary audience:** Who else might use it?
- **Demographics:** Age range, lifestyle, tech comfort level
- **Psychographics:** What motivates them? What frustrates them?
- **Usage context:** When and where do they use this app?

### 1.4 Write the Elevator Pitch

Compress the idea into 2-3 sentences that would make someone say "that's cool, tell me more."

**Framework:**
> "[App Name] is a [category] app that [core function]. Unlike [existing alternatives], it [key differentiator]. Built for [target audience] who want to [desired outcome]."

### 1.5 Draft the Vision Statement

The vision statement goes beyond what the app does today. It describes where the product could go.

**Include:**
- **Now:** What does v1 do?
- **Next (6-12 months):** What does the app become with traction?
- **Later (1-3 years):** What's the ambitious long-term vision?

### 1.6 Define Core Values / Principles

What principles guide design decisions when there are tradeoffs?

**Examples:**
- "Fun over accuracy" (a casual fitness app vs. a clinical tool)
- "Privacy first" (no social features that compromise data)
- "Simplicity over features" (do fewer things, but do them well)

These become the tiebreaker when two valid approaches conflict.

## Deliverable

Complete the [Vision Statement Template](../templates/vision-statement.md) and save it to your project's `docs/concept/vision-statement.md`.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Raw idea is written down
- [ ] Problem statement clearly identifies audience, problem, and current workaround
- [ ] Target audience is defined with specifics (not "everyone")
- [ ] Elevator pitch is 2-3 sentences and compelling
- [ ] Vision statement covers Now / Next / Later
- [ ] Core values/principles are defined (3-5 max)

## Tips

- **Talk it out.** Explain the idea to someone (or to Claude). If you struggle to explain it simply, the concept needs more refinement.
- **Don't optimize yet.** This step is about clarity, not perfection. You'll refine as you learn more in later steps.
- **Name it.** Even a working name helps make the idea feel real. You can change it later.
